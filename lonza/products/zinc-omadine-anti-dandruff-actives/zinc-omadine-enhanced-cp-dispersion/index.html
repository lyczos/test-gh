This method uses skin strippings as the growth medium for the yeast to simulate the growth conditions on the scalp. The skin strippings are treated with the test compound and then inoculated with a suspension of M. furfur and allowed to incubate at 27Âº C for 9 days. The samples are stained and viable yeast cells counted by computerized image analysis to determine growth of the M. furfur; the fewer viable yeast found, the greater the efficacy of the active to kill the organisms. Corneofungimetric evaluation of both the actives alone as well as in a shampoo formulation was performed.
